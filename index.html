Valentine's Day Spending Analysis

Exploring consumer spending patterns and trends across different demographics from 2010-2022

Historical Spending Trends by Category

Key Insights:

This line chart reveals the evolution of Valentine's Day spending across different gift categories from 2010 to 2022. Jewelry emerges as the highest-spending category, reaching nearly $46 per person in 2022, reflecting its status as a luxury item. Surprisingly, flowers rank lower than expected, trailing behind clothing, evenings out, and even gift cards. This suggests that while flowers remain a traditional choice, consumers are increasingly opting for more diverse and potentially more expensive gift options. The overall trend shows consistent growth across most categories, indicating rising Valentine's Day spending patterns over time.

Spending Patterns by Gender

Gender Analysis:

This horizontal bar chart compares spending preferences between men and women across different gift categories. Flowers emerge as the most popular category overall, with women spending significantly more on flowers than men. This finding is particularly interesting given the relatively low historical spending on flowers shown in the first graph. Jewelry ranks as the second most popular category, with men spending the most in this category, aligning with traditional gift-giving expectations. The data suggests that while both genders celebrate Valentine's Day, they have distinct spending preferences that reflect different gift-giving strategies and cultural expectations.

Age Group Spending Patterns

Age Demographics:

This heatmap visualization shows spending patterns across different age groups and gift categories. Jewelry consistently ranks as the highest-spending category across most age groups, confirming its status as a premium Valentine's Day gift. The heatmap reveals interesting age-based spending patterns, with certain age groups showing distinct preferences for specific categories. This visualization helps identify which age demographics are most likely to spend on particular gift types, providing valuable insights for understanding consumer behavior across different life stages and their Valentine's Day spending priorities.
